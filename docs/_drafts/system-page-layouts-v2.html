System Page — Minimal Layouts

Subtler, more distinctive, and using the full width — entries that read as part of the page, not boxes on it.

From your notes: drop the boxed cards (let entries blend into the page), use the space better, and lean into a unique, minimal structure. Four directions below, all in the dark palette, all box-free (separation is hairlines + typography + layout). Each handles navigation differently. Pick one and I'll build it as the real reader.

1

Lexicon

term-gutter · no separate rail needed

A definition-list spine: the entry name lives in a left gutter (right-aligned, so it sits against the body), the text flows in a wide column. The gutter of names is the index, so it doubles as navigation — no extra rail. Hairlines, not boxes. Reads like a scholarly lexicon and fills the width.

Blinded

#blinded
Cannot see and automatically fails any check that needs sight. Attacks against it have advantage; its own attacks have disadvantage.

Charmed

#charmed
Cannot attack the charmer or target it with harmful effects. The charmer has advantage on social checks against it.

Frightened

#frightened
Has disadvantage on checks and attacks while the source of its fear is in sight, and cannot willingly move closer to that source.

Prone

#prone
Can only crawl unless it stands. Disadvantage on attacks; melee attackers have advantage, ranged have disadvantage. Compare &condition[blinded].
2

Sticky Sidelabel

name pins beside its body

Each entry's name sits in a left column and sticks as you scroll its body — so the term you're reading is always labelled at your eye line, Tufte-sidenote style. A slim Contents rail on the right. Distinctive, calm, and box-free.

3

Margin Numerals

editorial index

Big, faint serif numerals in the margin (01, 02…) number each entry; the active one lights up. Gives rhythm and a sense of place in a long list without any chrome. Pairs with an optional right rail. Editorial and unmistakably ours.

Hairline Manuscript

purest minimal

A single narrow measure, entries divided only by a hairline and air, each with a tiny letterspaced kicker above a serif name. No columns, no boxes — just well-set type. The most restrained option; best for shorter, prose pages. (Right rail optional.)

My read

  • Lexicon (1) is my pick: most space-efficient, the name-gutter removes the need for a separate rail, and it reads as one continuous reference page rather than stacked cards. Scales cleanly to long lists.
  • Sticky Sidelabel (2) is the most distinctive interaction; Margin Numerals (3) the most editorial. Hairline Manuscript (4) is the most restrained for shorter pages.
  • All four are box-free and theme-adaptive (they'll render gold on the dark/parchment themes, blue on light). The page Description stays as a quiet lead at the top in every one.
