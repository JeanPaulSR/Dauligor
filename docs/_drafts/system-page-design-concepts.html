System Page — Reader Designs

Four ways to make /system/<page> worth landing on.

A system page is where a &condition[prone] reference takes you — a canonical glossary a player or DM reads, not just a data dump. The current build renders a flat list; below are four richer treatments using real condition content. Each is a static mockup — pick one (or a blend) and I'll build it as the real SystemPageGlossary. Sample entries are paraphrased, not full rules text. The refined direction from your feedback is shown first; the four initial explorations follow it for the record.

★

Refined: General Article + Blocks

the build target

A general article page: a prominent Description at the top — the content a page-level &condition reference shows, when you cite the system itself and not a specific entry — then each entry as a clean, generic block. No system-specific icons, so the exact same layout serves Conditions, Magic, or Homebrew. Article feel from concept 1, minus the thematic glyphs.

Description — shown for a page-level reference

Conditions are temporary states — imposed by spells, attacks, or the environment — that change what a creature can do until removed. Each entry below lists what it denies and what it allows.

Contents BlindedCharmedFrightenedProneStunned

Blinded #blinded

Can't see and auto-fails checks needing sight. Attacks against it have advantage; its own attacks have disadvantage.

Charmed #charmed

Can't attack the charmer or target them with harmful effects; the charmer has advantage on social checks against it.

Prone #prone

Can only crawl unless it stands. Disadvantage on attacks; melee attackers have advantage, ranged attackers have disadvantage. See also &condition[grappled].

Stunned #stunned

Incapacitated and can't move; auto-fails STR and DEX saves; attackers have advantage against it.

Indexed Codex

best for big glossaries

A sticky left index (jump-links, highlights the section you're in) beside the entries. Fast to scan 20+ conditions, and a &condition[prone] deep-link both scrolls and lights up its index row. Reference-doc clarity, lightly themed.

Card Gallery

set aside

Each entry is a card with its medallion, name, and a one-line summary. Great when the page is something you skim before diving in. A deep-link can highlight or expand the targeted card. Icon-forward and the most "at a glance".

3

Illuminated Tome

set aside

A single manuscript column — framed medallions, drop-caps, and gold flourishes between entries. Reads like a page out of an in-world rulebook. Beautiful for shorter, prose-heavy pages (rules, lore-rules); heavier to scan than the indexed layouts.

Conditions

The afflictions that may befall any creature, and what each denies them.

❖

Blinded

Robbed of sight, the creature fails any check that asks the eye to judge. Foes strike at it with advantage, while its own blows fall wide.

Prone

Brought low, it may only crawl until it rises. Those near enough to reach it gain advantage; those at range find it a poorer mark.

Stunned

Senseless and reeling, it cannot act or move, and its body will not answer a call for strength or grace. Every attacker presses the advantage.

4

Master & Detail

best for deep entries

A list on the left, the selected entry's full write-up on the right — like a wiki or inbox. A &condition[prone] deep-link opens straight to that entry. Ideal when entries are long (apply-effects, examples, sub-rules). Shown on the dark theme to demonstrate contrast.

Prone

A prone creature's only movement option is to crawl, unless it stands up and thereby ends the condition. It has disadvantage on attack rolls.

An attack roll against the creature has advantage if the attacker is within 5 feet; otherwise the attack roll has disadvantage. Related: &condition[grappled].

Where this lands

  • The refined direction at the top is the build target: a general article page — a prominent Description section (the page-level reference target) + generic entry blocks, with no system-specific icons.
  • Card Gallery (2) and Illuminated Tome (3) are set aside — too entry-decorative and not article-like.
  • To confirm before I build: keep the inline Contents jump-list (shown), or add a sticky side rail for long pages? And should entries support an optional image_url thumbnail (generic), or stay text-only?
