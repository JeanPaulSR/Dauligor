Crafting & Commerce The plain-words version

We're building three things that turn out to be one system: a way to make magic items, the ingredients and upgrades that go into them, and a shop where players buy and sell. Here's the whole picture without the technical vocabulary.

🧩 Four pieces, four everyday ideas

Ingredient

Crafting Material

Stuff you carry in your bag — dragon scales, a bar of mithral, a vial of essence. You collect it, stack it, and use it up when you make something.

Upgrade

Enchantment

A reusable power-up, like "Flame Tongue" (adds fire). You don't carry one around — you apply it. The same enchantment can go on a sword, an axe, or a dagger.

Finished product

Magic Item

What you get when an upgrade is put on a base item: an ordinary greatsword + "Flame Tongue" = a flaming greatsword. These will eventually get their own tab.

The store

Shop

A place to see prices and buy or sell. Looking at prices is easy (items already have them); actually spending money needs one thing we don't have yet — see below.

💡 The one idea that shaped the plan

A material is a thing you hold. An enchantment is something you apply. They feel similar ("both are stuff in the compendium"), but that one difference decides how the app stores each one — so we treat them differently on purpose.

Because a material is a real object you carry, we let it ride on the app's normal "item" machinery (so it drops onto a character sheet like anything else) and add a thin crafting label on top. Because an enchantment is not a carried object, it gets its own dedicated list — a library of upgrades you can apply to many different base items.

✅ What we decided

  • Enchantments get their own library. The good news: the app already knows how to describe an enchantment's effect (the same tool that handles other special abilities handles these), so we're reusing that rather than building it twice.
  • Materials are normal items with a crafting tag. In the virtual tabletop (Foundry) they'll show up as their own "crafting material" category — and conveniently, that category already exists there, so they'll slot in cleanly.
  • First, build the two libraries — nothing more. Just the catalogs of enchantments and materials. The actual "combine them into something" crafting logic, the magic items tab, and the shop all come after, each as its own step.

📜 The piece that ties it together: recipes

A recipe is just what it sounds like — a card that says "combine these ingredients (and maybe a base item and an upgrade) to make this result." It turns out to be the single way everything gets made: potions, refined materials, magic items, and even the upgrades (enchantments) themselves. That's why we're building the recipe screen first — it's the engine the rest plugs into. (You just decided this — recipes are the foundation.)

🪙 The one missing piece for the shop

Right now characters don't actually have a coin purse in the app — there's no place that tracks how much gold someone is carrying. Browsing a shop's prices works without it, but buying and selling can't happen until we add one. So the shop will start as a "window-shopping" view and gain real transactions once the purse exists.

🧹 A small housekeeping note

Separately, I left a reminder for myself to tidy up something invisible to you: the app has two slightly different internal naming styles for older vs. newer data, and there's a little translation layer bridging them. Once we standardize the older parts, that translator can be deleted — fewer moving parts, fewer bugs. It's written down so it doesn't get forgotten; it doesn't change anything you see.
