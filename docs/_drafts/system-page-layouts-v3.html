System Page — Subtle Blocks

Entries as blocks again — but quiet ones that belong to the page, not cards sitting on top of it.

v2 dissolved the entries into a list; you want blocks, just subtle. So v3 keeps a block per entry — each is still a distinct zone you can anchor to — while removing the hard card edges. The page reads as one article: a quiet italic description lead at the top, then blocks set off by soft fills, a spine, or a rule instead of borders. All dark-theme, all use the width, slim right rail for jumping.

1

Soft Inset

blocks, no edges

Each entry is a softly-filled zone — a faint wash a touch lighter than the page, rounded, roomy — with no border. Present as a block, but it melts into the page; brightens slightly on hover. The most literal read of "make the blocks subtle."

System Page
Conditions

Temporary states — imposed by spells, attacks, or the environment — that change what a creature can do until they end.

Blinded

Cannot see and automatically fails any check that needs sight. Attacks against it have advantage; its own attacks have disadvantage.

Charmed

Cannot attack the charmer or target it with harmful effects. The charmer has advantage on social ability checks against it.

Frightened

Has disadvantage on checks and attacks while the source of its fear is in sight, and cannot willingly move closer to that source.

Prone

Can only crawl unless it stands. Disadvantage on attacks; melee attackers have advantage, ranged have disadvantage. Compare &condition[blinded].

Accent Spine

threaded, no fills

A single hairline runs down the left; every block hangs off it with a small accent node at its name. No fills, no boxes — the spine is what groups them, so it stays airy while clearly being a sequence of blocks.

3

Ruled Header

article sections

No block container at all — each entry is a section whose name carries a short accent underline, separated by whitespace. The "block" is implied by the heading + space. Reads exactly like a well-set article; the most minimal while still feeling sectioned.

Quiet Focus

chrome only on focus

Blocks are flat by default — just a name and body with breathing room. A soft fill + left accent appears only on hover or when targeted (so a &condition[prone] deep-link lights its block up on arrival). Calmest at rest, with a clear "you are here". Prone below is shown active.

My read

  • Soft Inset (1) is the most direct answer to your note — clearly blocks, clearly subtle (fill, no edges).
  • Quiet Focus (4) is the same idea but even quieter, lighting up only the entry you land on — great with deep-links.
  • Accent Spine (2) threads them without any fill; Ruled Header (3) is the most article-pure (sections, not containers).
  • All keep the quiet italic Description lead and the slim right rail, and adapt to gold (dark/parchment) / blue (light).
