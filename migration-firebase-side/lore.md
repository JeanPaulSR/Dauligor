"Cagosia is a land gripped by near-eternal winter. Its summers bring little relief, only a light dusting of snow in place of warmth, and its winters are brutal, marked by frozen rivers, blinding blizzards, and bone-deep cold. Locals say the frost drifts down from the Kathrax Mountains to the west, and that the cold worsens whenever Halmat shifts in his slumber. The people of Cagosia have grown hard beneath this relentless winter. Thicker skin and heavier clothing help, but survival owes as much to the region’s light industrialization, fueled by coal and rich metal veins drawn from the Kathrax mountains. The cities have adapted. Factories hum in the snow-choked streets, and massive generators pump out precious heat to keep the frost at bay. But the warmth of the cities is a thin veil. Beyond their borders, the cold reigns unchecked. Small towns and outposts survive only through constant fuel gathering and sheer determination. The deeper into the wilds one travels, the more hostile the land becomes. The Guild once tried to establish a major presence here, but the blistering cold drove most adventurers south. For many, the choice between freezing wilderness and the cramped safety of a heated city is no choice at all. The result? The wilds belong to monsters. Yet monsters are not the only threats lurking in the snow. Winter has claimed entire noble lines, their halls left empty and unguarded. In the void, bandits, thugs, and would-be lords have risen, carving out power wherever the law is slow to reach. To the north lies Shia, a constant thorn in Cagosia’s side. Their raids are near clockwork, waves of warriors, frost mages, and shamans descending from the north in search of land, food, and power. Though repelled time and again, their presence, along with the bitter memory of Alunis’ invasion, has soured the people’s view of magic in all forms. And further north lies Shia. One of the greatest sources of pain for Cagosia throughout its history. Raiders, aided by frost mages and shamans, have like clockwork attempted to take the lands of Cagosia by force and were repelled each time. Their raids, alongside an unforgotten attempted invasion by Alunis, has created a distance of magic in all its forms. To the point of being seen as taboo in some places, or outlawed outright. Those who practice magic in Cagosia must do so in secret. Open casting draws the attention of the Witch Hunters—agents of the Mandate, trained to root out magic and its corruption. Once marked, few escape their notice. Even adventuring parties willing to turn a blind eye know the truth: those who draw the attention of Witch Hunters only delay the inevitable."
